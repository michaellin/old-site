Feedback Control

Michael Lin, Emily Chen, Franklin Gu

UC Berkeley, Fall 2013

This was one of my favorite classes at UC Berkeley. Besides the controls theory taught in class, it also included a lab section which was almost magical.

First lab project we had was to build a closed-loop controller to perform magnetic levitation on electromagnets to be able to levitate metal objects. This is a video of the controller working (video credits to Emily).

Second project only got more exciting. Our goal was to program a PID controller to move a cart with a hinged pendulum such that the pendulum is maintained upright. Here is the video of it working (Again credits to Emily).

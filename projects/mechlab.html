Mechatronics Design Lab

Michael Lin, Emily Chen, Maruchi Kim

UC Berkeley, Spring 2014

This was a semester long project with the course EE 192 (Mechatronics Design Lab) at UC Berkeley. The project consisted on designing and building an autonomous racing car that is able to follow a racing track marked with tape. The semester culminated with a national competition at UC Davis (NATCAR), where my team won first place with an average speed of 9.06 feet/s.

This is video of the car running:

Our project made use of a line array camera for detecting the tape on the racing track

The motor controller board was custom built which integrates a double H-bridge and boost converters to provide maximum power to the motors efficiently.

We used a Freescale FRDM-KL25Z microcontroller (ARM Cortex-M0) to do the sensor data filtering and real time control of the car.
